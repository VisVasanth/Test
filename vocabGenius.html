VOCAB GENIUS MOBILE APPLICATION
COMPLETE UX PROCESS


The Vocab Genius is a mobile application that was designed to empower people on the go to learn and study new vocabulary whether it was for personal or for a standardized exam (ie. GRE, SAT, GMAT).


Problem

Many people want an easy effective way to study vocabulary on the go without needing to change too much of their current routine in order to reach their professional goals.

Process Overview


For Vocab Genius, as you can see, we did the complete design thinking process on this application from understanding our users to presenting our findings and designs. Conducting competitive analysis, user interviews, persona creation, and task analyisis with user flow was crucial before even starting the wireframes and prototypes. I chose to do low-fideltity sketched wireframes, which were easily converted by Prott into clickable protptypes for usability testing. Iterations of usability testing allowed me to better understand, which aspects of my design were user friendly and which areas needed more improvements.

DELIVERABLES:
Competitive Analysis, User Interviews, Personas, Task Analysis, User Flows, Wireframes & Prototypes, Usability Testing, and Presentation


Phase 1: UNDERSTAND
Competitive Analysis

For the competitive analysis, I analyzed 3 competitors: GMAT Vocab 4800 , WordsApp: Vocabulary Builder , and the Magoosh SAT Flashcards: Prep & Vocab . After analyzing the various pros and cons of each app, I referred back to this data during the wireframing phase.

❮ ❯

Phase 2: OBSERVE
User Interviews

By conducting user interviews, I was able to gather qualitative data and to truly understand the users of the vocabulary application. I was able to better understand what my users do, think, and feel in regards to studying as well as their motivations, goals, and needs.

Phase 3: POV
Personas

Conducting the user interviews and analyzing the data allowed me to create an accurate proto-persona for Julie H. She is a 27 year old who lives in New York City. She is a successful Senior Associate Management Consultant who often has to travel for work. She has a supportive husband and a 2 year old son Jayden. Julie’s goal is to study for her GMATs in order to attend a great MBA program so she can move up the corporate ladder at work and eventually make partner.

Julie needs a tool that will have all the study materials she needs while allowing her to study and quiz herself on the go. She also needs a tool to remind her to study and keep her on her study schedule with short increment study sessions.



Phase 4: IDEATE
Information Architecture
Task Analysis and User Flows

After understanding our user’s goals, needs, and motivation, I moved on to completing user flows and task analyses for some of the tasks that the Julie & our users would want to complete. The tasks that I chose to analyze were Creating an Account, Playing the 5 minute Quiz Game, and Setting Study Reminders. This analysis allowed us to create more organized and user centered wireframes.





Phase 5-6: WIREFRAMING, PROTOTYPING, USABILITY TESTS

After using parts of the Time-blocking strategies for rapid prototyping, I was able to create my initial sketches. For this specific application, I felt that using a Flat Navigation would be more appropriate as it allows users to easily switch between multiple categories. I hand drew all the screens and used PROTT to create my sketches into clickable tests for usability testing. I created screens for:

  • SPLASH
  • TUTORIAL
  • LOG IN/SIGN-UP
  • MY ADMIN/PROFILE
  • STUDYING
  • UPLOADING A WORD
  • TESTING YOUR KNOWLEDGE
  • SETTING STUDY REMINDERS

Once I completed the wireframes, I decided to run usability tests. I completed 3 sessions of approx 10 minutes each and used the Jakob Nielson’s Error Severity Rating Scale as our metrics. Our users performed the following 5 tasks:

1. Read through the tutorial.
2. Create a new account.
3. Add a new word.
4. Quiz yourself via the game.
5. Set a study reminder.


TASK 1: COMPLETE TUTORIAL

Two-thirds of users were confused on how to exit the tutorial at the end.

I will change the “Skip” button on the last tutorial screen to “Exit”.


TASK 2: CREATE ACCOUNT

Users were able to successfully complete task.

“All you had to do was read and click.” - Uma


TASK 3: ADD NEW WORD

Two-thirds of users had difficulty locating the ”Add a word button”. Once they were able to locate it, they easily completed the task.

"I was annoyed that it was so hard to find the add word button” –Uma

The navigation bar icon for add word was not clear for the user so I will be adding labels and maybe changing the icon.


TASK 4: QUIZ YOURSELF VIA 5 MINUTE QUIZ GAME

Initially, users had difficulty figuring out what icon to click to get to the quiz game. However, was then able to easily complete task.

The navigation bar icons at the bottom weren’t always clear for the user so I will be adding labels and maybe changing an icon or two.


TASK 5: ADD A REMINDER

Two-thirds of users mentioned that it is not clear which button to click to add a new study reminder. In addition, users wanted more information on the “auto populate” and “motivation” options.

To address this, I will change the top + button to read “Add Reminder”. To address the second issue, I will keep the ‘information’ buttons and add detailed description pop up/modals.

Lessons Learned

User research, user flows, and task analyses allowed me to have a clear idea on what tasks the users would complete with the Vocab Genius Mobile Application. Doing that part of the UX process made it easier and more organized to develop my initial sketches.

Using Prott was a great way to turn simple low fidelity wireframe sketches into clickable prototypes for usability testing. I also learned the significance and value in usability testing. It was eye opening to learn how users navigate through my designs and what issues they ran into. Using Prott and low-fidelity prototypes save a lot of time because changes are easier to make to the prototypes with each iteration of usability testing.

Lastly, I learned the importance of iterating through the design process as you are constantly learning more and more about your users.
